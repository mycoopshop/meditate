SUMMARY

- Acceptance in this context means being willing to experience your unwanted feelings for what they are. This is the art of allowing.

- This is never about trying to change your feelings. Allowing means changing the relationship you have with your unwanted feelings, from resistance to acceptance, without directly changing them.

- We can use mindfulness to drop the resistance within.

- Sometimes we can’t stop the inner resistance that we’re experiencing. But resistance doesn't have to be debilitating. We can still take meaningful action when we’re aware of inner resistance, even though we would rather it not be there.

- You can be accepting of the parts that are not accepting; you can be accepting of your resistance.

- Mindfulness provides us with a gentle, all-encompassing container for whatever we’re carrying on the inside.

- 'What am I noticing?' is a simple but powerful tool to return to the I that observes.

- You are not your experiences. You're the witness to them.

- The I in you that observes is pure awareness. Pure awareness simply is.

- This I can’t be hurt, manipulated or affected in any way. It simply observes. It can’t judge our experiences as good or bad because judgements are thoughts.
