BE HERE NOW... AND MAYBE WITH A FRIEND

What about introducing a good friend to mindfulness?

And you could be the guide.

This is a great exercise where you can practise guiding a friend, or vice versa. If you want, you could just read the following script until you’re familiar with the basics, and then close your eyes and follow the instructions accordingly. It’s also worth remembering that there are no hard and fast rules to mindfulness, so the script can vary according to you.

Before starting this exercise, it’s helpful to know that distractions will come from your mind, and that's perfectly normal.

If you're doing this for yourself, read it slowly and take in every word.

Allow yourself to do as the script says.

Be here now guided meditation script

Find a comfortable position in which you can remain relaxed, yet alert. Simply become aware of noticing. (5 secs)

Ask yourself, 'What am I noticing right here, right now?' (10 secs)

Reclaim awareness from your mind and the busy world of doing. Allow yourself to directly sense how gravity is impacting you in how you are resting. (10 secs)

Each time you are distracted, gently return your awareness to how you make contact with where you are resting. (10 secs)

Gently bring awareness to your hands, feeling the warmth from within them. (10 secs)

Let your fingertips gently touch together. Notice how this feels. (10 secs)

Can you feel the tiny grooves of your fingerprints? (10 secs)

Now return your hands to where they were. (5 secs)

Become aware of what your fingers are touching now. (10 secs)

Can you tune into the delicacy of what you are sensing? (10 secs)

Bring attention to your hands and arms. (10 secs)

Notice any areas which are tense or tingling. (10 secs)

Explore the experiences of your hands and arms as you feel gravity weighing down upon them. (10 secs)

Now move awareness to your feet and give your toes a wiggle. (10 secs)

Bring awareness to your legs as well. (10 secs)

Notice how you are resting. Become fully aware of your chest, your back and your belly. (10 secs)

Notice how you are breathing. Place one hand on your chest and one hand on your stomach. Is your chest moving more than your belly? (10 secs)

Bring your awareness to your face. Notice your forehead. Can it relax some more, or are you aware of it being fully relaxed? (10 secs)

Move to the muscles around your eyes. Notice their softness. (10 secs)

Let awareness flow into your face as a whole. (10 secs)

Notice the stillness of your mouth and tongue. (10 secs)

Now bring your awareness to the angles of your arms and legs. (15 secs)

Flood your whole body with awareness. (15 secs)

Now think about this room that you’re in right now. Where are you positioned? What is around you? (15 secs)

Can you sense your body as a whole in the space of this room? (15 secs)

See if you can keep some mindful awareness as you open your eyes and come back to the room, noticing what it is that you see. (30 secs)

As this meditation is coming to an end, notice the subtle ways your body responds in preparation for this. (30 secs)

Know that you can return to mindful awareness at any time during your day with a shift to deliberate attention.
