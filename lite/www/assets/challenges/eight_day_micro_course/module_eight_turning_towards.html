3) Today's life experiment

There are two facets of mindfulness. Conscious awareness is one. The second is the art of allowing. In this context, it is not about accepting things in the external world, but about accepting things in our internal world. This is because it's our inner world that gets affected by our external world.

How am I going to do this? Where am I going to do this? When am I going to do this?

Remember How? Where? When? for any one life experiment that you choose from the list below:

Experiment A: The next time you feel fear, anger and frustration. Hold yourself lightly. The more you open to it, the less it will push you around.

Experiment B: Acknowledge the situations where you can stop the struggle within. When you can't do this, observe the part of you that is resisting. Stay with it for a few moments.

Experiment C: Come to know that you always have control of your actions, even if your mind says that you don't. Be brave and experiment with something that your mind says you can't or shouldn't do. Choose one task that would give you a sense of meaning that your mind says no to. Notice how your mind can still say no whilst you're actually doing the task.

Experiment D: Next time somebody says something that pushes your buttons, take a moment to notice the tendency you have to counter-attack. Doing so means you're likely to continue having the same response in future situations. If you can take a moment, ask yourself honestly and openly, What does this situation say about me (instead of them)? What can I learn about myself here, for my own self-growth? Notice the tendency you may have for your mind to stop you from doing this for 'better' reasons! Gently turn towards yourself, notice the aversion, and ask.
