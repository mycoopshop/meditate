3) Today's life experiment

Mindfulness helps with nurturing our intention behind connecting to another person. When we connect in this way it makes our interactions with others more meaningful.

How am I going to do this? Where am I going to do this? When am I going to do this?

Remember How? Where? When? for any one life experiment that you choose from the list below:

Experiment A: Before going into a conversation that could easily be a mindless or automatic chit-chat, take a moment to notice what you’re thinking, feeling and experiencing in the external world. Then gently engage mindfully by either focusing on the sound of the person’s voice, or noticing the subtleties of their facial expressions as they talk to you.

Experiment B: Upon your return home, give your loved one 5 minutes of your undivided attention.

Experiment C: The next time someone is talking to you, in person or on the phone, drop a gentle reminder to yourself: What if this was the last time I'm ever going to see this person (because it could be). Notice any changes in how you interact with them.

Experiment D: Having completed the Mindfulness of the Breath (11 mins) guided meditation, which specific aspect of the breath were you more easily drawn to? At some point today when you feel the need to reconnect, use this specific point of focus as a tool to help keep you grounded.
