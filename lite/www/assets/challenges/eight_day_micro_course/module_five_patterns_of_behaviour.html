3) Today's life experiment

We’re talking about patterns of behaviour. We all have patterns of behaviour with varying degrees of frequency. Some are important to come to know. Some are helpful. They move us towards a meaningful life. Some are not. They steer us away from a meaningful life.

How am I going to do this? Where am I going to do this? When am I going to do this?

Remember How? Where? When? for any one life experiment that you choose from the list below:

Experiment A: Reflect upon some of the little patterns of behaviour you do that don't really serve you. Make a little list of some of the ones you would like to change. When you find yourself doing them, see if you can do them mindfully, instead of mindlessly. That's it. Don't even think about changing them yet.

Experiment B: Make it a habit to notice the difference between what your mind tells you (instant gratification is good) and what your experience of life tells you (instant gratification isn't always helpful). Ask yourself in the moment: Right now, this is what my mind is saying, but what does my experience tell me?

Experiment C: Notice which situations trigger emotional discomfort, whether it’s among certain family members, friends or particular environments. You'll feel more rigid, controlling and unaccepting of others. Work with the situations that cause a subtle reaction within you. Start by simply becoming aware, and if you can, take some gentle curiosity to yourself. As you do this, you’ll notice how there's an option to be more fluid, flexible and accepting of how you feel.

And…

Becoming more aware of your patterns of behaviour is important. This is about overcoming your conditioning. It's self-growth. Bringing this to your awareness is the first step towards change.

Mindfulness opens the capacity within us for getting unstuck from these unhelpful little patterns of behaviour because it makes space for our emotions and this changes the relationship we have to them.
