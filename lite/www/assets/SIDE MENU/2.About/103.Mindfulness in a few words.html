But what is mindfulness?

Mindfulness is about being open and receptive to all your experiences, including what's going on inside of you. You'll take less interest in your thoughts, unless they’re helpful. It’s about getting out of your mind and into your life.

What’s fascinating is that in recent years, neuroscience has shown that mindfulness makes visible changes to the structure of your brain. Certain areas actually appear thicker in brain scans.

There are also well over 2,000 research studies demonstrating how effective it is. It’s been shown to benefit those who suffer from chronic pain, to help reduce stress, to strengthen the immune system, to reduce symptoms of depression and anxiety, and to help people lead happier and more purposeful lives.

Many of these scientific studies were of people who practised for just 20 minutes daily.
