What’s the common relationship with you and your unhelpful thoughts? Do you respond in the two most popular but often most unhelpful ways? Let's take a look…

Do you have a tendency to believe ('buy') your unhelpful thoughts? Or do you have more of a tendency to resist and reject ('battle') them? Let’s have a look at the deal we get with these approaches.

Buy?

When we buy our unwanted thoughts we believe the words they're advertising to us. They take us over and give us the experience of looking at the world through tinted glasses. For example, it could be looking through the lens of Not good enough! This makes the world appear to be negative. This is because we're looking from our unhelpful thoughts, instead of at them.

Battle?

Okay, so battle may be a strong word here. I chose it because it is like a mini battle within us sometimes. When we battle our unwanted thoughts, we try not to think them or distract ourselves in an attempt to get rid of them. A lot of the time this can be so subtle that we're not even aware that we're doing it. The things we do to distract ourselves from these thoughts can work, but usually in the short term only. This is because when they return, we're likely to have exactly the same response to them. Another mini battle.

The question is this: Can we control our unhelpful thoughts?

Whether we 'buy into' our unhelpful thoughts or 'battle' them by trying hard not to think about them, either way, we actually direct energy towards them. This gives them more power. This often results in them coming back, and in some cases, coming back stronger.

So, let's have a little recap here. When we buy into our unwanted thoughts, we believe them. When we battle our unwanted thoughts (as in trying hard not to think about them or by distracting ourselves in an attempt to get rid of them), they come back. These approaches give thoughts more energy and they can come back stronger.

Whether we buy in to or battle our thoughts, we provide them with more energy.
