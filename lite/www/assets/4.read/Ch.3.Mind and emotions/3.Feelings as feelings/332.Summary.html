SUMMARY

- Our emotions and urges are not always in alignment with who we really are.

- We can be 'programmed' to resist and suppress desirable emotions such as love and curiosity, as well as the undesirable ones.

- Resistance is a normal defence mechanism that begins in early childhood and helps children stabilise undesirable emotions. But when used excessively, it can cause problems.

- Resistance to undesirable feelings is the driving force behind our reactive behaviours.

- To be unwilling to experience any emotion causes resistance which distorts it.

- When we resist our emotions, we have a tendency to react in unhelpful ways.

- Mindfulness gives our emotions space. This gives us choice instead of being controlled by them.

- In the struggle with resistance, we react. In the power of willingness, we respond.
