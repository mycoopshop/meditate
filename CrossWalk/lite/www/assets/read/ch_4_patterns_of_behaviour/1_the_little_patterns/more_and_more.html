MORE AND MORE

When we're caught up in little patterns of behaviour that don't really serve us, it's easy to get pulled into more of doing the same.

It’s like all these patterns are done in the service of 'fixing' ourselves, again and again, more and more, of 'fixing' the unrest within. But consider this:

What actually needs fixing?

Nothing. We’re talking about a feeling of unrest within. A feeling we really can’t be with. But, is this really true? Mindfulness helps us recognise that it isn’t. We have the direct experience of being open and receptive to what the feeling is really like, by gently turning towards it.

Perhaps the biggest tragedy of our lives is that freedom is possible, yet we can pass our years trapped in the same old patterns. - Tara Brach

As this quote by Tara suggests, these little patterns can become bigger patterns. Without mindfulness, we may continue with these for a very long time. It could be anyone. For example, it’s possible to have a big family, a large network of friends and an abundance of material possessions, and yet still feel that there’s something missing; still struggling with an undercurrent of unrest within.

Many people are on antidepressants because of this. Life becomes entrenched in more: more medication, more material things, more quick-fix experiences that are merely distractions. And yet, the more we obey our thoughts that serve to fix us, the more we can feel disconnected from our sense of self and others.

It can come to a point where a good question to ask ourselves is:

Does this life belong to me, or does it belong to the thoughts and feelings I've picked up along the way?

This question shows the divide that we have within us from time to time. There’s often a conflict. Our thoughts and feelings (that is to say our conditioning from past experiences) can sometimes pull us away from what really matters.

In the heat of the moment, our minds tell us:
Doing things for instant gratification feels better. Just do it!

Experience shows us that:
Doing things for instant gratification doesn’t move us towards a meaningful life.

Because of this, it’s no surprise to think:

Geez! What's wrong with me? …

… when you recognise a familiar pattern playing out in your life and you're feeling the division within you. These are the moments when we give ourselves a hard time for what we're thinking and how we're acting. If you can relate to this, just know that …

There's nothing wrong with you. You're not broken or in need of fixing. You may simply be stuck.
